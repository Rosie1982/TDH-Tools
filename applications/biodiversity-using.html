How to use this tool

CliMAS Biodiversity shows the relationship between biodiversity patterns and climate currently, and how this may change in the future

Viewing biodiversity patterns

CliMAS Biodiversity starts by showing you a menu that allows you to choose between vertebrate classes: birds, mammals, amphibians and reptiles.

Once you have selected a class, a menu that allows you to choose families or genera will become active. If you choose family or genera, a drop down menu will appear that will allow your to choose a family or genera.

Select a year.

To view our best estimate of the current trajectory, use the default emissions scenario (RCP8.5).

The biodiversity map will be displayed as a popup.

Viewing effect of greenhouse gas emissions on biodiversity patterns

CliMAS Biodiversity not only allows you to view changes in patterns over time, it displays interaction between time and different emissions trajectories. To view how much a decrease in emissions can impact the future of biodiversity, select any of the lower emissions scenarios (RCP2.6, RCP4.5, or RCP6).
